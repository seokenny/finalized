HealCo

Shared medical space made simple

“Whether you're listing your medical space or looking to lease, we make sharing simple.”

Overview

HealCo is a web platform made for medical hosts and guests to lease or rent a space based on their specialties. It allows users to easily enlist their properties while using a matching system to connect lessors with the perfect lessee and vice versa. HealCo also has a huge focus on telemedicine - a way for patients to get medical assistance through digital and long distance means. HealCo is the Airbnb of medical office space and ambulatory surgery centers - and now the only official Health System Without Walls (HSWOW).

Goals

One of the main reasons I was brought into HealCo was to redesign the entire user interface and user experience for their website, dashboard, and form flows. As HealCo was still a relatively new company, most of their tasks were focused on the completion of its core features. This left the design at a bare minimum. I organized the sections by order of importance and began my redesigns. One thing I had to keep in mind was scalability, as HealCo was still rapidly rolling out new features for its users.

Order of Goals

I laid out the goals that I needed to fulfill based on user flow direction:

  1. Website: Being relatively new, HealCo received its users through connection in the medical field and word of mouth. Due to that, its users already knew what HealCo provided without having to look through the website for information. However, this creates a lack of information for completely new users who find HealCo through other means.
  2. Form Flows: When users began the sign up process, we found out that users were leaving mid process due to specific questions. Some were unanswerable or frustrating to answer, but was required.
  3. Dashboard: As more features are added to the dashboard, there was a severe lack of direction and smooth navigation for users. Users have said it was difficult to navigate and required many clicks to reach their destination. This was apparent in both the dashboard’s design and its navigation system. Options were too deeply hidden without a quick and simple way to be led to the source of information.
Users

The majority of the users were hosts who were looking to rent out their medically approved space, guests who were looking for medical equipment provided space, and the admins of HealCo who mediated the information and requests behind the platform. While the hosts and guests have a similar experience, I had to keep in mind the extra features that the admins would have.

Roles

I work closely with 4 other full stack engineers while I am the sole designer. The engineers have their own features they are all working on individually, but keep everyone in the loop when we have our weekly standup. I work closely with each of them to completely understand the features they are working on while also providing my mock ups and designs to get feedback.

Website Redesign

The biggest issue with HealCo's website was the lack of information. Users had no idea what HealCo did, how it worked, and whether or not HealCo was a legitimate service. Before coming up with a design, I figured out the questions that users ask before committing to a product or service. This was how I would determine the necessary information that the website would provide to the users.

For the landing page, I made sure to add as much information as possible to give the users the information they needed while providing extra to give them confidence in HealCo. I broke down the features and information that was the most noted by users:

  • HealCo's purpose and mission - so users know they’re joining a platform who knows the wants of its users and fulfills them
  • How HealCo works - knowing upfront what HealCo does with crystal clear understanding so users know what service they’re receiving and knowing if HealCo is what they need
  • Testimonies - to build trust, security, reliability for users interested in signing up for HealCo’s services
  • Mentions from reputable sources - as HealCo is a relatively new company, it is important to reference articles and mentions from reputable sources (like Forbes) to show its legitimacy
Website Redesign Conclusion

With the new landing, we’re able to showcase all the crucial information that users look for when signing up for a new service. It showcases all the initial information about HealCo while giving it’s users confidence in HealCo’s purpose, reliability, and trust.

Form Flows Redesign

The main and biggest complaint was the amount of information required to finish signing up. Some of the questions were too specific, unknown or annoying for users to receive immediately, such as room dimensions in square feet, budgeting, and staff / room information. Users felt lost and frustrated when asked such specific questions before they could even get to the dashboard to see what HealCo had to offer. This led to some users leaving the form mid-way through the process.

Fixing the issue

For the main issue of requiring too much specific information, I first laid out the entire flow process and marked them by importance. Besides the mandatory question of personal information, I kept the questions that gave a general idea of what the user was looking for. I was able to remove half of the questions asked but still kept enough information for HealCo to categorize and create personalized dashboards and results for each user.

Dashboard Redesign

I laid out every feature and it’s flow for all users - Admins, Hosts, and Guests. I realized the lack of connections and links between information, making the user go through the entire dashboard to find the correlation information. I included many necessary hyperlinks for users to click if they wanted more information. I also added a visual of progression so users knew how they were doing in the current flow they were in. One thing I focused on was creating a sense of flow between each feature for the users to follow through. This helped users complete each step without being lost on what they’re required to do next.

Other Designs

On the side, I created graphics for HealCo's resources and articles page. I used isometrics to keep a sleek, clean design and used HealCo's color pallete plus some complementary colors to keep a consistent yet vibrant look.

Conclusion

With the lack of necessary information and clear sense of division between different options, it gave users a confusing and frustrating user experience from start to finish. Showing a clear indication of the users category made them know they were going through the process correctly. The new form flows prevented users from leaving mid sign up because of unnecessarily complicated requests. With the new FTUE, it guided new users through their personalized dashboard which helps users use their features to the fullest. And finally with the new dashboard, it was easier than ever for users to navigate their dashboard. This smooth experience from start to finish gives users confidence in themselves and the platform that they’re using.
